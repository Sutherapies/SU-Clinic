Understanding the Pessary: A Simple Support System for Your Pelvic Floor If you're exploring non-surgical options for pelvic floor concerns, you may have come across the term pessary. Put simply, it’s a soft, flexible device made from medical-grade silicone. The best way to think of it is like a sports support or an orthotic, but designed specifically to help your internal pelvic organs. This support is essentially a prosthetic device inserted into the vagina to provide gentle, structural reinforcement. It works by carefully lifting and holding your pelvic organs—such as the bladder, uterus, or bowel—in their correct position. This can immediately alleviate the feelings of pressure, bulging, and discomfort often associated with prolapse. To help you visualise how this works, this short video offers a clear explanation: What Does a Pessary Help With? A correctly fitted device is a versatile and effective tool for managing a range of pelvic health conditions. A specialist physiotherapist may recommend one to help with: Pelvic Organ Prolapse (POP): Providing reliable support for a descended bladder (cystocele), rectum (rectocele), or uterus (uterine prolapse). Stress Urinary Incontinence (SUI): Reducing or stopping bladder leaks that happen when you cough, sneeze, laugh, or exercise. Temporary Support: Offering extra stability during pregnancy or in the postpartum period as your body recovers safely. How is a Pessary Different from Other Devices? It's important to understand that a pessary serves a unique therapeutic purpose. Unlike a menstrual cup, which is designed to collect fluid, its function is to provide physical support. It is also different from a contraceptive diaphragm, which is used for birth control. A correctly fitted pessary is a long-term management tool, designed to be worn comfortably for extended periods to help you regain confidence and get back to your daily activities without discomfort or surgery. Common Types of Pessaries: Finding the Right Shape for You One of the most common questions we hear is, “Which pessary is right for me?” The answer is that it’s deeply personal. Pessaries come in a wide variety of shapes and sizes, each designed to support different types of prolapse and suit unique anatomies. While the number of options can seem daunting, a specialist physiotherapist is expertly trained to assess your specific needs and find the perfect fit for your body. While a clinical overview of pessary use shows dozens of designs, most women find lasting comfort and relief with one of a few common types. The goal is always to find a solution that is so comfortable and effective, you can forget it’s there and get on with your life. The Ring Pessary Often the first choice for managing mild to moderate prolapse, the ring pessary is simple and effective. As the name suggests, it is a soft, flexible O-shaped ring that can be folded for gentle insertion. Once inside, it opens up to provide foundational support to the vaginal walls. Some ring types are designed so that you can learn to remove and re-insert them yourself at home, offering greater flexibility for activities like intercourse. The Gellhorn or Cube Pessary When a more significant prolapse requires greater support, a Gellhorn or Cube pessary may be recommended. These are designed to hold their position more securely. The Gellhorn uses a broad, supportive base and a small stem to hold everything in place, while the Cube uses the gentle suction of its concave sides to adhere to the vaginal walls. Because of their design, these types are almost always managed by your healthcare provider, who will remove, clean, and refit them for you at regular appointments. Other Specialised Shapes Beyond these common options, there are many other specialised shapes, such as the Donut (a thicker, rounder ring for more severe prolapse) or the Hodge (which is shaped specifically to support the bladder). However, the specific name or shape of the pessary is far less important than the expertise of the fitting. Our priority is to work with you to find the device that provides the best support with the most comfort, giving you the confidence to return to the activities you love. Frequently Asked Questions About Pessaries Will I be able to feel the pessary once it's in? A correctly fitted pessary should be completely comfortable, and you shouldn't be aware of it as you go about your day. It sits high in the vaginal canal, away from the more sensitive lower areas. If you can feel it, or if it causes any discomfort, it may not be the right size or shape for you. It’s important to contact your practitioner for a review so they can make the necessary adjustments to ensure a comfortable fit. Can a pessary fall out when I go to the toilet? While it is uncommon, a pessary can sometimes be expelled, particularly if you are straining to open your bowels. A well-fitted device should stay securely in place during normal toilet use. If your pessary does fall out, don't panic. You can wash it with soap and water and reinsert it if you have been shown how. Otherwise, simply store it safely and contact your practitioner to arrange for it to be refitted as soon as possible. Are there any side effects or risks I should know about? Pessaries are a very safe treatment, but like any medical device, there are potential side effects. The most common is an increase in vaginal discharge, which is usually harmless. Less frequently, some women may experience a slight odour, irritation, or minor bleeding. Regular follow-up appointments with your specialist are essential to monitor for any issues and ensure your vaginal health is maintained, significantly minimising any risks associated with long-term use. How long can a pessary be used for? Do they need replacing? A pessary can be a long-term solution for managing prolapse for many years. Depending on the type and your personal circumstances, it will be removed, cleaned, and reinserted by your practitioner every 4-6 months. During these appointments, the device is checked for any signs of wear. While they are made from durable medical-grade silicone, they are typically replaced every 12 months to ensure they remain effective and hygienic. Can you have sex while wearing a pessary? This depends on the type of pessary you have. Many designs, such as a ring pessary, do not block the vaginal canal and can be left in place during sexual intercourse, often without you or your partner noticing. However, other space-filling types, like a cube or Gellhorn pessary, must be removed beforehand. We encourage you to have an open conversation with your practitioner about your lifestyle so they can help you choose the most suitable option. Who is the best professional to fit a pessary: a GP, gynaecologist, or a specialist physio? In the UK, pessaries can be fitted by a specially trained GP, a gynaecologist, or a specialist pelvic health physiotherapist. While all are qualified, a specialist physio or gynaecologist often has more extensive, dedicated training in fitting a wide range of devices. A pelvic health physio can also provide a holistic treatment plan, combining the fitting with a personalised pelvic floor exercise programme to achieve the best possible outcome for you. Does using a pessary mean I can stop doing my pelvic floor exercises? Absolutely not. We view a pessary and pelvic floor exercises as a team working together to manage your prolapse. The pessary provides physical support to your organs, reducing your symptoms and allowing you to exercise more comfortably and effectively. Continuing with your tailored exercises is vital for strengthening your pelvic floor muscles, which provides active, long-term support. Combining both gives you the best chance of regaining control and confidence.
