Antenatal & Pregnancy Physiotherapy

Supporting a healthy and comfortable pregnancy

SU Women’s Health Physiotherapy & Wellness – Manchester

Pregnancy brings many physical changes that can sometimes lead to discomfort, pain, or pelvic floor concerns. At SU Women’s Health Physiotherapy & Wellness, we provide specialist antenatal physiotherapy to support women through every stage of pregnancy.

Our pregnancy care focuses on helping you stay active, comfortable, and confident while preparing your body for labour and recovery after birth.

Antenatal Physiotherapy

Antenatal physiotherapy supports the musculoskeletal changes that occur during pregnancy. As the body adapts to support your growing baby, many women experience pain, postural changes, or pelvic floor concerns.

Physiotherapy during pregnancy can help manage symptoms safely while improving strength and mobility.

Common conditions we treat

  • Pelvic girdle pain (PGP)
  • Lower back pain
  • Sciatica
  • Hip pain
  • Pelvic floor weakness
  • Urinary leakage during pregnancy
  • Diastasis recti (abdominal separation)
  • Rib and mid-back pain
  • Postural discomfort

Birth Preparation Physiotherapy

Preparing your body for labour is an important part of antenatal care. Birth preparation physiotherapy focuses on strengthening key muscles and improving mobility while teaching relaxation and breathing techniques for labour.

Birth preparation sessions may include

  • Pelvic floor strengthening and relaxation
  • Breathing techniques for labour
  • Pelvic mobility exercises
  • Perineal preparation guidance
  • Optimal labour positioning advice
  • Strategies to reduce risk of birth injury

Pregnancy Massage

Pregnancy massage is designed to safely relieve tension and discomfort during pregnancy. It helps improve circulation, reduce muscle tightness, and promote relaxation.

Pregnancy massage may help with

  • Lower back pain
  • Pelvic and hip discomfort
  • Neck and shoulder tension
  • Leg cramps
  • Swelling in legs and feet
  • Stress and fatigue
  • Sleep difficulties

Pelvic Floor Health During Pregnancy

The pelvic floor muscles support the bladder, bowel, and uterus. During pregnancy these muscles are placed under increased pressure and may become weak or less coordinated.

Pelvic floor physiotherapy helps maintain strength and function of these muscles, supporting both pregnancy and recovery after birth.

  • Pelvic floor assessment
  • Individualised pelvic floor exercises
  • Bladder and bowel health advice
  • Pelvic floor relaxation for labour

Benefits of Pregnancy Physiotherapy

  • Reduced back and pelvic pain
  • Improved posture and movement
  • Better pelvic floor strength
  • Preparation for labour
  • Increased comfort during pregnancy
  • Faster recovery after birth

Who Can Benefit?

  • Women experiencing pregnancy-related pain
  • First-time mothers seeking guidance
  • Women with previous pelvic floor issues
  • Women preparing for labour
  • Women wanting to stay active safely during pregnancy
