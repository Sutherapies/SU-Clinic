Pregnancy and childbirth change the body far more than most women are prepared for. Many women are told symptoms such as leaking urine, abdominal weakness, heaviness or discomfort are “normal after a baby”. These symptoms are common — but they are treatable.

A postnatal physiotherapy assessment is a detailed check of your recovery after pregnancy and delivery. The aim is to restore pelvic floor function, rebuild abdominal strength and help you return to normal activity and exercise safely and confidently.

Suitable from 6 weeks after a vaginal or caesarean birth — and also months or years later if symptoms persist.

Why a postnatal check is important

During pregnancy and birth, the pelvic floor muscles, abdominal wall and supporting tissues stretch considerably. Even an uncomplicated delivery can affect the structures that support the bladder, bowel and pelvic organs.

Without assessment, many women unknowingly:

  • return to exercise too early or progress too quickly
  • perform unsuitable core exercises that worsen symptoms
  • overload healing tissues and delay recovery
  • worsen pelvic heaviness or prolapse symptoms
  • develop ongoing bladder symptoms

Early assessment can reduce the risk of long-term pelvic floor dysfunction and help you feel confident in your body again.

What we assess during your appointment

Pelvic floor assessment

We assess pelvic floor strength, endurance and coordination — including your ability to contract and relax. Pelvic floor dysfunction can contribute to:

  • urinary leakage when coughing, sneezing or exercising
  • urgency / frequent urination
  • heaviness or pressure
  • reduced control or confidence

An internal assessment may be offered (always optional and explained fully beforehand) as it provides the most accurate diagnosis and rehab plan.

Tummy gap (diastasis recti) & core control

A “tummy gap” is common after pregnancy. We assess the width and depth of separation, abdominal muscle control, and whether you have doming/coning with movement.

  • diastasis recti assessment
  • deep core coordination and breathing
  • safe abdominal exercise guidance

Bladder health

We support common postnatal bladder symptoms, including:

  • stress incontinence (leaking with cough/jump/run)
  • urgency and urge leakage
  • frequent urination
  • feeling you can’t fully empty

Bowel health

We can help with postnatal bowel changes such as:

  • constipation and straining
  • difficulty emptying
  • wind control issues
  • anal discomfort

Prolapse screening

We check for signs of pelvic organ prolapse and provide early management. Symptoms may include:

  • heaviness or dragging sensation
  • pressure or bulging sensation
  • discomfort with standing, walking or lifting

Posture, breathing & movement

Pregnancy can alter posture and breathing patterns, affecting pelvic floor and abdominal function. We assess:

  • posture and rib position
  • breathing mechanics and pressure management
  • lifting technique (baby/pram/car seat)

Safe return to exercise and activity

Many women feel unsure when it is safe to return to exercise. We provide guidance specific to your body and delivery type, helping you return to activity without worsening symptoms.

We advise on safe return to:

  • walking and general fitness
  • gym training and strength work
  • running (including return-to-run planning)
  • Pilates, yoga and low-impact classes
  • lifting and high-impact activity

Returning too early can overload healing tissues. A tailored plan prevents setbacks and builds confidence.

Who should book a postnatal physiotherapy assessment?

You may benefit if you experience:

  • leaking urine
  • tummy gap / diastasis recti / doming
  • abdominal weakness or instability
  • pelvic heaviness, pressure or prolapse concerns
  • pain with intimacy
  • back or pelvic pain
  • fear of returning to exercise
  • slow recovery after caesarean birth
  • feeling “not quite right” after childbirth

Even if you have no symptoms, a check can provide reassurance and help prevent future issues.

What you will receive

After your assessment you will receive:

  • a clear explanation of your recovery
  • a personalised pelvic floor programme
  • safe abdominal/core exercises for diastasis recti
  • a return-to-exercise plan tailored to your goals
  • practical advice for day-to-day life (lifting, posture, breathing)

Frequently asked questions

Is an internal examination compulsory?

No. It is optional and only offered with your consent. You can decline at any time.

Is it painful?

No. Any examination is gentle and explained throughout in a private clinical setting.

How long is the appointment?

Typically around 60 minutes.

How soon after birth can I attend?

Usually from 6 weeks postpartum, or earlier for advice if needed.
