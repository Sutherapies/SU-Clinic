What is Pelvic Organ Prolapse?

Pelvic organ prolapse occurs when the muscles, ligaments and connective tissues that support the pelvic organs become weakened or stretched. As a result, one or more pelvic organs, including the bladder, uterus, bowel or the top of the vagina, may descend into the vaginal canal, causing a feeling of pressure, heaviness or a vaginal bulge.

Pelvic organ prolapse is a common condition that can affect women of all ages. It is more frequently seen following pregnancy and childbirth, during or after menopause, after pelvic surgery or as part of the natural ageing process. Although common, prolapse is not something you simply have to live with, and many women benefit from specialist conservative treatment.

Common Symptoms of Pelvic Organ Prolapse

  • A feeling of heaviness or dragging within the pelvis
  • A sensation of a vaginal bulge or something "coming down"
  • Bladder leakage or difficulty emptying the bladder
  • Difficulty emptying the bowel completely
  • Pelvic discomfort during walking or prolonged standing
  • Symptoms that worsen towards the end of the day
  • Reduced confidence during exercise or lifting
  • Discomfort affecting work, hobbies or everyday activities

Every woman's symptoms are different. Some women experience only mild discomfort, while others find that prolapse significantly affects their daily activities and quality of life. Following a thorough assessment, we will discuss the most appropriate treatment options to help you manage your symptoms and achieve your personal goals.

What is a Vaginal Pessary?

A vaginal pessary is a removable medical device designed to support the pelvic organs and relieve the symptoms of pelvic organ prolapse. It is inserted into the vagina and provides gentle support to the bladder, uterus, bowel or vaginal walls, helping reduce discomfort and improve quality of life.

Pessaries have been used safely for many years and are recognised as an effective non-surgical treatment for many women with pelvic organ prolapse. They can also help selected women with stress urinary incontinence by providing additional support to the bladder neck during physical activity.

When correctly fitted, a pessary should feel comfortable and allow you to continue most of your normal daily activities with little or no awareness that it is in place.

A pessary may be suitable if you:

  • Wish to avoid or delay prolapse surgery
  • Are waiting for surgical treatment
  • Want to remain physically active
  • Experience prolapse symptoms during exercise or prolonged standing
  • Prefer a conservative treatment approach
  • Are not medically suitable for surgery
  • Want additional pelvic support during everyday activities
  • Would like to improve your comfort, confidence and quality of life

Choosing the right pessary is important. The correct type and size depend on your anatomy, the type and severity of prolapse, your lifestyle and your personal goals. This is why every pessary fitting at SU Women's Health begins with a comprehensive pelvic health assessment to ensure the most appropriate option is selected for you.

Our Specialist Pessary Fitting Process

Every woman is unique, which is why there is no "one-size-fits-all" pessary. Our specialist pessary fitting process is designed to ensure you receive the most appropriate pessary for your anatomy, symptoms, lifestyle and personal goals while maximising comfort and long-term success.

1. Comprehensive Pelvic Health Assessment

Your appointment begins with a detailed discussion about your symptoms, medical history, bladder and bowel function, previous treatments, lifestyle, physical activity and personal goals. This allows us to understand how prolapse is affecting your daily life and determine whether a pessary is the most appropriate treatment option.

2. Specialist Pelvic Examination

With your consent, a gentle pelvic examination is performed to assess the type and severity of prolapse, vaginal tissue health, pelvic floor muscle function and vaginal dimensions. This information helps us select the most suitable pessary for your individual needs.

3. Choosing the Right Pessary

We provide a wide range of vaginal pessaries and carefully select the most appropriate type and size based on your anatomy, symptoms, activity levels, lifestyle and treatment goals. Choosing the correct pessary is essential for comfort, effectiveness and long-term success.

4. Pessary Fitting & Comfort Assessment

Once fitted, we assess the pessary for comfort, support and stability. You may be asked to walk around, sit, stand or empty your bladder to ensure the pessary remains comfortable during everyday activities.

5. Education & Self-Management

Before you leave, we provide personalised advice on pessary care, physical activity, bladder and bowel habits, pelvic floor exercises, lifestyle modifications and, where appropriate, self-management techniques. We want you to feel informed and confident in managing your pessary.

6. Follow-Up & Ongoing Care

Regular follow-up appointments are an important part of successful pessary management. These reviews allow us to check that your pessary remains comfortable and effective, monitor vaginal tissue health, answer any questions and make adjustments if required.

Our goal is not simply to fit a pessary but to provide ongoing support that helps you remain active, comfortable and confident for the long term.

Types of Pessaries We Fit

There is no single pessary that is suitable for every woman. The most appropriate pessary depends on the type and severity of prolapse, your anatomy, symptoms, lifestyle, activity levels and personal preferences. During your assessment, we will recommend the option that is most likely to provide effective support while remaining comfortable for everyday use.

Pessary Type Commonly Used For
Ring Pessary Mild to moderate pelvic organ prolapse. Comfortable, easy to manage and suitable for many women.
Ring Pessary with Support Provides additional support for women with bladder prolapse (cystocele) or more significant prolapse symptoms.
Shaatz Pessary Suitable for selected women with uterine prolapse or prolapse requiring greater support than a standard ring pessary.
Cube Pessary Often used for more advanced prolapse when additional support is required.
Gellhorn Pessary Provides firm support for advanced pelvic organ prolapse and is commonly managed with regular clinical follow-up.
Donut Pessary Designed for women with larger or more complex prolapse requiring increased support.

If one pessary type is not suitable or comfortable, this does not mean pessary treatment has failed. There are many different shapes and sizes available, and finding the most appropriate option may occasionally require adjustment. Our goal is to provide a comfortable, effective solution that supports your lifestyle and helps you remain active and confident.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does pessary fitting hurt?

Most women find pessary fitting comfortable and well tolerated. You may feel some pressure during the examination and fitting, but it should not be painful. We take time to ensure you feel relaxed throughout the appointment.

How long does the appointment take?

Your initial appointment usually lasts around 45–60 minutes, allowing enough time for a detailed assessment, pelvic examination, pessary fitting (if appropriate) and personalised advice.

Do I need a GP referral?

No. You can book directly with us without a GP referral.

Can I exercise with a pessary?

Yes. Many women find they can return to walking, gym exercise and other physical activities more comfortably after a correctly fitted pessary. We also provide advice on safe exercise and pelvic floor rehabilitation.

Can I remove my pessary myself?

This depends on the type of pessary fitted. Some women are able to remove and reinsert their pessary independently, while others prefer regular follow-up appointments for ongoing management. We will discuss the most suitable option for you.

How often does my pessary need to be checked?

Follow-up appointments vary depending on the type of pessary and your individual circumstances. We will recommend a review schedule to ensure your pessary remains comfortable, effective and safe.

What if the first pessary doesn't fit?

Finding the right pessary sometimes requires trying a different size or type. We offer a wide range of pessaries and will work with you to find the option that provides the best support and comfort.

Can a pessary prevent surgery?

Many women successfully manage their symptoms with a pessary for years. While a pessary does not cure prolapse, it can significantly improve symptoms and may delay or remove the need for surgery for some women.

Can I have a pessary if I have had a hysterectomy?

Yes. Many women with vaginal vault prolapse following a hysterectomy can benefit from pessary treatment. During your assessment, we will determine the most appropriate option for your condition.

What should I bring to my appointment?

Please bring details of any medications, previous pelvic surgery, relevant medical history and any questions you would like to discuss. Wearing comfortable clothing can also make your visit easier.
