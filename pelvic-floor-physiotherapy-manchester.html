What is Pelvic Floor Physiotherapy?

Pelvic floor physiotherapy focuses on assessment and treatment of the muscles that support the bladder, bowel and uterus. Weakness, tension or dysfunction in these muscles can lead to symptoms such as incontinence, pelvic pain or prolapse.

Symptoms Pelvic Floor Physiotherapy Can Help With

  • Urinary leakage when coughing, sneezing or exercising
  • A feeling of heaviness or pressure in the pelvis
  • Pelvic pain
  • Difficulty controlling the bladder or bowel
  • Postnatal pelvic floor weakness
  • Pregnancy-related pelvic discomfort

Your Pelvic Floor Assessment

Your appointment includes a full pelvic health assessment, discussion of your symptoms and goals, and a personalised treatment plan. Treatment may include pelvic floor muscle training, education, lifestyle advice and rehabilitation exercises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral for pelvic floor physiotherapy?

No referral is required. You can book directly with SU Women’s Health Physiotherapy & Wellness in Manchester.

When should I see a pelvic floor physiotherapist?

You may benefit from pelvic floor physiotherapy if you experience urinary leakage, pelvic pressure, pelvic pain, or symptoms after pregnancy and childbirth.

How many physiotherapy sessions will I need?

The number of sessions varies depending on your condition and goals. Many women start with an assessment followed by a personalised treatment plan.
