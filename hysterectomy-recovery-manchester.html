Hysterectomy Recovery: How Women's Health Physiotherapy Can Help

A hysterectomy is one of the most common gynaecological operations performed in the UK. Whether your surgery was recommended for fibroids, endometriosis, adenomyosis, heavy bleeding, prolapse or another condition, recovery can take time and patience.

Many women expect the surgery itself to solve their symptoms but are often surprised by the physical and emotional challenges that can occur during recovery. Specialist Women's Health Physiotherapy can help you recover safely, regain confidence and return to the activities you enjoy.

What Is a Hysterectomy?

A hysterectomy is a surgical procedure to remove the uterus (womb). Depending on the reason for surgery, it may be performed through an abdominal incision, vaginal surgery or laparoscopic (keyhole) surgery.

Recovery varies from person to person, but it is common to experience changes in strength, mobility and pelvic floor function during the healing process.

Common Problems After Hysterectomy

  • Pelvic floor weakness
  • Urinary leakage or urgency
  • Abdominal weakness
  • Lower back pain
  • Pelvic discomfort
  • Scar tissue tightness
  • Reduced confidence with exercise
  • Fatigue

How Can Women's Health Physiotherapy Help?

Pelvic Floor Rehabilitation

The pelvic floor muscles support the bladder, bowel and pelvic organs. Following surgery these muscles can become weak or uncoordinated. Specialist assessment and rehabilitation can help improve strength, control and confidence.

Core Strengthening

Your abdominal and deep core muscles play an important role in supporting the spine and pelvis. A tailored rehabilitation programme can help restore strength and improve movement.

Scar Management

Scar tissue can sometimes feel tight or uncomfortable. Physiotherapy can help improve tissue mobility and reduce restrictions.

Returning to Exercise

Many women are unsure when it is safe to return to the gym, running or other forms of exercise. Physiotherapy can provide a gradual and safe progression based on your recovery.

When Should You See a Women's Health Physiotherapist?

You may benefit from physiotherapy before surgery, during early recovery or even months and years after a hysterectomy. It is never too late to seek support for ongoing symptoms.
